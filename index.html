What is this site?

This is a guide to Berkeley Student Cooperative (BSC) budgets from FY 2007-08 through FY 2026-27, built for board directors. It compiles data from multiple board-approved budget spreadsheets.

📋
Actuals for completed years: Figures for FY 2007-08 through FY 2025-26 are actuals drawn from the prior-year columns in each BSC budget file (which reflect real Accufund accounting data). FY 2026-27 is a board-approved projection — that fiscal year has not yet concluded.
🏠
What is the BSC? The Berkeley Student Cooperative is a student-run nonprofit providing affordable housing and meals to UC Berkeley students since 1933. Members govern directly and contribute labor (workshifts) for below-market rates. It is financially self-sustaining — no university subsidy. The BSC operates 17 co-op houses plus Rochdale, Fenwick, and Northside apartments, serving ~1,200+ members.

The structural deficit: The BSC has run deficits since FY 2021-22. The Evans Manor/Barrington lease ended, eliminating $434K/yr in income. Expenses continue to outpace revenue. This budget does not contribute to reserves, scholarship, growth, or contingency funds.

Budgeted occupancy assumptions

When building the FY 2026-27 budget, the VPCF set occupancy assumptions based on prior years' patterns. These assumptions determine how much room and board revenue the budget projects — if actual occupancy falls short, revenue falls short too, and the deficit widens further.

Summer 2026 — budget assumption43%
Fall 2026 — budget assumption90%
Spring 2027 — budget assumption84.75%

Summer is structurally low every year — members leave for internships, travel, or home. Fall is always the revenue engine. Spring sees some attrition. These percentages were set in April 2026 using historical patterns and determine the revenue line in the budget.

Trends over the years

How key budget lines have moved over 20 years — FY 2007-08 through the FY 2026-27 projection, using data from the Previous Budgets spreadsheet (2007-2022) and the YoY sheets (2023-2027). The only gap is FY 2022-23, where we have total actuals but not individual line items. These trends reveal the structural cost pressures building beneath the surface.

Staff Payroll & Benefits

+87%

2007-08 → 2026-27 · Board-capped at $4.15M this year

The BSC's largest single expense. The 2024-25 spike (+20% in one year) prompted the Board to cap payroll at $4.15M for FY 2026-27. Includes all wages, taxes, and benefits for ~30+ staff.

Insurance

+163%

2007-08 → 2026-27 · Now essentially flat year-over-year

Insurance costs more than doubled over the decade — a nationwide trend for housing nonprofits. The past two years have stabilized, which is a meaningful positive sign.

Legal Fees

+317%

2007-08 → 2026-27 · $60K → $250K · Every year rising since 2022

The sharpest cost escalation in the budget. Legal fees have risen every single year since 2023-24, driven by evictions, member delinquency disputes, and regulatory compliance. From a rounding-error line to a top-10 expense in 10 years.

Gas & Electricity

+22%

2007-08 → 2026-27 · Was higher in 2011-14; recently re-accelerating

Energy costs have risen steadily for a decade, with a sharp jump projected for FY 2026-27 (+22%). The BSC's 17 large old houses are energy-intensive and difficult to retrofit economically.

Water

+212%

2007-08 → 2026-27 · $216K → $676K · Steepest recent acceleration

Water rates in Berkeley have risen dramatically. The BSC's large communal kitchens and shared bathrooms mean consumption per-unit is higher than typical apartments. A persistent structural pressure with no easy lever to pull.

Financing (Debt Service)

+103%

2007-08 → peak 2011 · then falling · $1.60M in 2023-24 → $1.19M projected 2026-27

Debt service is declining as BSC loans mature and get paid off. This is genuinely good news — the BSC is reducing its debt burden. If this trend continues, it will free up meaningful cash in future budgets.

Room & Board Rate vs. Room Revenue — the occupancy gap

Rate +47% since 2016-17 · Room Revenue only +13% over same period

2016-17 → 2026-27 · Rates rising much faster than actual revenue collected

R&B Rate per semester

Total Room Revenue

Despite raising the per-semester rate 47% since 2016-17, total room revenue has only grown 13% — because occupancy has not kept pace. The BSC is charging members more per bed but filling fewer of them, especially in recent years. This gap is a core driver of the structural deficit.

Glossary

Workshift

Required labor hours each member contributes weekly — cooking, cleaning, maintenance. Typically 5 hrs/week. This is what keeps BSC costs below market rates.

CFS

Cooperative Food System — the BSC's centralized food procurement and distribution network serving all houses.

Capital Improvement

Spending on building upgrades, repairs, and renovations. Separate from routine maintenance. FY 2026-27 focus: HiP and Rochdale roof repairs.

Financing

Debt service — interest and principal payments on BSC loans and mortgages. Declining as debts are paid down.

Bad Debts

Provision for members who don't pay their bills. Up 121% in FY 2026-27 as a liquidity buffer.

VPCF

Vice President of Capital & Finance — the elected board member who authors the annual budget. FY 2026-27: Adrian Meneses (Euclid).

Evans Manor / Barrington

A property previously leased to the BSC, generating ~$434K/yr in lease income. The lease ended, eliminating this revenue stream.

Convent Rate

A monthly parking rate ($30/mo FY2016-17 → $49.69/mo projected FY2026-27) for the BSC-leased Convent property. Not a housing rate. Lease expired 12/31/2026.

Hillegass Parker (HiP)

Hillegass Parker House — a BSC co-op limited to graduate and re-entry (age 25+) students. 57 residents, all singles (33 standard, 24 deluxe).

Form 990

Annual IRS filing required of nonprofits. 990 figures differ from operating budgets — they use GAAP accounting (capitalizing capital expenses, including investment income).

Accufund

The BSC's accounting software. The prior-year columns in each annual budget file pull from Accufund and represent real actuals.

NSC

Northside Cooperative — 26 residents in 1BR apartments and studios near the north side of campus. Graduate/re-entry focused. Waitlisted — requires 2+ semesters of BSC membership.

IRS Form 990 vs. operating budget

⚠️
Why 990 and budget figures differ: The 990 (GAAP) includes investment income, donations, and asset sales in revenue, but capitalizes capital expenditures rather than expensing them. Operating budgets include capital projects and debt payments as expenses but exclude investment/donation income. Both are correct — they answer different questions. The 990 almost always shows a surplus even when the operating budget shows a deficit.
